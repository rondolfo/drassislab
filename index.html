About

I am a biologist, physiologist, and herpetologist exploring the interrelationships between stress, behavior and immunity. I graduated in Biological Sciences from UNESP (2007), earned my Master of Science in General Physiology from USP (2010), completed my PhD in Sciences (General Physiology) also from USP (2015), and concluded a Post-Doctorate at USP (2020).

My research focuses on ecoimmunology, stress endocrinology, conservation, behavior, and reproductive physiology — primarily in amphibians. I use approaches such as transdermal corticosterone application to simulate changes related to acute stress responses, restraint challenges, and immunological challenges. Currently at UWA, I am investigating the causative agent of Lyme disease (Borrelia burgdorferi) using epigenetic and immune mechanisms in Peromyscus mice.

Research

🐸

Amphibian Ecoimmunology

Stress-induced immunomodulation in amphibians: acute vs. chronic stressors, corticosterone effects on cellular and humoral immunity.

🧪

Stress Endocrinology

HPI axis activation, corticosterone and testosterone plasma dynamics, transdermal hormone application as experimental tool.

🦠

Disease & Conservation

Chytridiomycosis, Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis (Bd), ranavirus, and amphibian population declines driven by climate change.

🐭

Lyme Disease (Current)

Investigating Borrelia burgdorferi using epigenetic and immune mechanisms in Peromyscus leucopus and P. maniculatus at UWA.

🌎

Invasive Species

Stress and immune response profiles in invasive cane toads (Rhinella marina) in Florida compared to native species.

📊

Neuroimmune-Endocrine

Integrative approach linking behavior, reproductive endocrinology, and immune response in anurans using phagocytosis and NLR markers.
